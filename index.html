Walt Disney World Timeline

1971 - 2016

Click on the buttons above to filter by category and toggle categories. Click on any event for more information.

  • October 1, 1971: Magic Kingdom Opens

    Opened as the first theme park at Walt Disney World, Magic Kingdom is dedicated to fairy tales and Disney characters. The park is represented by its central icon, Cinderella Castle, and is laid out in a hub-and-spokes configuration, similar to Disneyland. The park consists of seven lands: Main Street, U.S.A., Adventureland, Frontierland, Liberty Square, Fantasyland, and Tomorrowland.

  • October 1, 1971: Disney's Contemporary Resort Opens

    This resort, easily identified by its A-frame main building, was one of two that opened with the Magic Kingdom in 1971. Located adjacent to the Magic Kingdom (and easily accessible by monorail or walking path), it features a modern, contemporary design theme, and contains a multi-story mosaic designed by Disney Legend Mary Blair. There are currently several restaurants at the Contemporary Resort, including California Grill, a signature restaurant located on the resort's top floor.

  • October 1, 1971: Disney's Polynesian Village Resort Opens

    This resort, one of two that opened with Magic Kingdom in 1971, features a South Seas theme. Known as the Polynesian Resort from 1985 to 2014, this resort has undergone a number of renovations and updates, including the addition of several new longhouses. A recent addition included the opening of Trader Sam's Grog Grotto, a tiki-themed bar featuring signature drinks and special effects, inspired by a similar bar at the Disneyland Hotel in California.

  • October 2, 1971: Admiral Joe Fowler Riverboat Begins Operation

    This steamboat, named for a retired Naval Admiral who played an important part in the building of Walt Disney World, ran on the Rivers of America in Magic Kingdom's Liberty Square until 1980, when it was damaged in a dry dock. Since a second steamboat had since been added to the Magic Kingdom, Disney decided to break up the Fowler for scrap.

  • October 14, 1971: 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea: Submarine Voyage Opens

    This attraction featured passenger vehicles based on the Nautilus design from the 1954 Disney adaptation of 20,000 Leagues Under The Sea, where passengers were seated below the water level and were able to view underwater show scenes. In different parts of the attraction, guests could see moray eels, crabs, lobsters, sea bass, clams, turtle, smaller tropical fish, shipwrecks, deepwater fish, a sea serpent, mermaids, and a giant squid. The attraction temporarily closed in 1994 without any advance notice, with the closure officially made permanent in 1996.

  • October 25, 1971: Electrical Water Pageant Debuts

    This nightly (weather permitting) show takes place on Walt Disney World's Seven Seas Lagoon and Bay Lake. It features a number of light-up floats that depict images of sea creatures, accompanied by various Disney songs. The show can be viewed from the Bay Lake area resorts or from in front of the Magic Kingdom.

  • November 19, 1971: Disney's Fort Wilderness Resort & Campground Opens

    This resort, located in the Magic Kingdom area, contains both campsites and cabins. The resort is home to the Hoop-Dee-Doo Musical Revue all-you-can-eat show, as well as other dining options. Entertainment options include trail rides, carriage and hay rides, a petting zoo, and the Campfire Sing-Along with Chip 'n' Dale.

  • June 5, 1972: If You Had Wings Opens

    This two-person Omnimover dark ride attraction, located in Magic Kingdom's Tomorrowland, featured travel destinations throughout the Caribbean and elsewhere. The ride had a popular eponymous theme song by Buddy Baker. The attraction was sponsored by Eastern Airlines, and would close in 1987, shortly after the airline withdrew its sponsorship. A slightly changed version of the attraction, called If You Could Fly, opened on June 6, 1987, but it would close less than two years later, in January 1989.

  • May 20, 1973: Tom Sawyer Island Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This attraction, located in Magic Kingdom's Fronteirland, is accessed by raft and contains structures and caves with references to Mark Twain characters from the novel The Adventures of Tom Sawyer.

  • May 20, 1973: Richard F. Irvine Riverboat Opens

    This steamboat was the second riverboat to enter into service on the Rivers of America at Magic Kingdom Park. Named after Disney executive Richard Irvine, the boat was renamed to the Liberty Belle Riverboat in 1996.

  • May 20, 1973: Swan Boats Open

    These boats took guests on a ride around the hub of the Magic Kingdom and the Swiss Family Treehouse. The original fleet of 12 boats was reduced to 5 boats before the attraction closed in August 1983 due to high maintenance and operation costs.

  • December 15, 1973: Pirates of the Caribbean Opens in Adventureland

    This dark boat ride, which inspired the film series of the same name, tells the story of a band of pirates and their troubles and exploits. The ride gave rise to the song "Yo Ho (A Pirate's Life for Me)" written by George Bruns and Xavier Atencio, and since 2006, has contained references to the film series (including several audio animatronics of Captain Jack Sparrow).

  • April 8, 1974: Treasure Island Opens

    This island, later renamed Discovery Island, was a zoological park located in Walt Disney World's Bay Lake. It was open to guests as an attraction, where they could observe its many species of animals and birds. After Discovery Island closed in April 1999, the animals were moved to Disney's Animal Kingdom, and the island was allowed to deteriorate.

  • November 28, 1974: Star Jets Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This rocket-spinner attraction, opened as part of a massive expansion in Magic Kingdom's Tomorrowland. The attraction involves rockets spinning around a central monument, with each of 12 open-air vehicles holding up to two passengers who circled round and round, 60 feet above the ground, while controlling their ascent and descent with a metal control stick. The attraction underwent a major redesign in 1994 and reopened with the name Astro Orbiter.

  • January 15, 1975: Walt Disney's Carousel of Progress Opens in Tomorrowland

    This attraction, created by Walt Disney and WED Enterprises for the General Electric (GE) Pavilion at the 1964 New York World's Fair, was located at Disneyland from 1967 until 1973, when it was closed and moved to Florida. The attraction's premise is an exploration of the joys of living through the advent of electricity and other technological advances during the 20th century via a "typical" American family and features the Sherman Brothers' song "There's a Great Big Beautiful Tomorrow."

  • January 15, 1975: Space Mountain Opens

    This indoor outer space-themed steel roller coaster, located in Magic Kingdom's Tomorrowland, is the oldest operating roller coaster in the state of Florida.

  • June 7, 1975: Mission to Mars Opens

    This show, located in Magic Kingdom's Tomorrowland, was designed in cooperation with NASA. Guests were "launched" on a spacecraft into space and approached the surface of the red planet Mars. The attraction closed on October 4, 1993 and was replaced by ExtraTERRORestrial Alien Encounter.

  • July 1, 1975: WEDWay PeopleMover Opens

    This attraction, themed as an urban mass transit system of the future, the ride takes passengers on a tour around the second floor of many attractions in Tomorrowland. Riders will also see a large diorama containing a portion of the Progress City or "E.P.C.O.T." model, views into several attractions, and a diorama of a futuristic hair salon. The attraction has undergone several refurbishments and name changes, and is currently called the Tomorrowland Transit Authority PeopleMover.

  • June 20, 1976: Disney's River Country Opens

    Disney's first water park, River Country featured a rustic wilderness theming, complete with rocks and man-made boulders. The park featured five water slides, two pools, and two children's areas. The park never reopened after its regular end-of-season closure in November 2001, and on January 20, 2005, The Walt Disney Company announced that the park was permanently closed. The park has since been left to deteriorate.

  • June 11, 1977: Main Street Electrical Parade Debuts

    This parade, which made its original debut at Disneyland in 1972 and opened at Walt Disney World five years later, featured floats and live performers covered in thousands of electronically controlled lights and a synchronized soundtrack triggered by radio control along key areas of the parade route. The parade has had several incarnations at Disney World, making its original run from 1977-1991, returning from 1999-2001, and making its most recent run from 2010 to 2016, when it closed to return to Disneyland.

  • November 15, 1980: Big Thunder Mountain Railroad Opens

    A combinations of turns and drops takes riders through an abandoned mine town on this mine train roller coaster, located in Frontierland at Magic Kingdom. The rides has two tracks with slightly different ride experiences.

  • October 1, 1981: Epcot Opens

    Epcot, the second theme park to open at Walt Disney World, is dedicated to the celebration of human achievement, namely technological innovation and international culture. The park is divided into two sections: Future World, containing a variety of pavilions that explore innovative aspects and applications including technology and science, and World Showcase, an area reminiscent of a permanent world's fair and consisting of pavillions themed and dedicated to represent a specific country.

  • December 15, 1987: Journey into Imagination Opens at Epcot

    This attraction, created by imagineers Tony Baxter and Steve Kirk, featured Dreamfinder, a pilot who uses his vehicle to collect dreams and ideas to create all sorts of new things. He creates a figment of his imagination, a purple and yellow dragon named Figment. Dreamfinder and Figment imagine new ideas and use them to interact with the world around them. At the end, Dreamfinder tells Figment and the audience that Imagination is the key to unlocking the hidden wonders of the world. The attraction closed on October 10, 1998 and was replaced by another imagination-themed attraction, Journey into Your Imagination.

  • June 9, 1984: Laserphonic Fantasy Debuts

    This nighttime show featured lasers emitted from barges and from around the World Showcase lagoon. The show, which replaced A New World Fantasy, closed in 1988 and was replaced by similar shows, Illuminations and then Illuminations 25, before finally being replaced by IllumiNations: Reflections of Earth in 1999.

  • September 7, 1984: Morocco Pavilion Opens in Epcot's World Showcase

    The Morocco Pavilion, the first expansion pavilion to be added to World Showcase, was designed to look like a Moroccan city with a realistic Minaret. It is the only pavilion in which the country's government aided in the design, as King Hassan II sent Moroccan artisans to design and create the many mosaics. Due to Islamic religious beliefs on the content of art, the mosaics contain no representations of people, and the pavilion's buildings do not light up during nighttime shows.

  • December 15, 1987: Magic Journeys Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This 3D film, which had previously been located at Epcot (1982-1986), looked at the world through the eyes of a child. It was directed by Murray Lerner and featured a song composed by Robert and Richard Sherman. The show closed in December 1993.

  • June 3, 1988: Norway Pavilion Opens at Epcot

    This World Showcase pavilion, situated between the Mexican and Chinese pavilions, is designed to look like a Norwegian village. The village includes a detailed Stave church, Restaurant Akershus (the exterior of which resembles its namesake in Oslo), and a series of interconnected shops decorated with large wooden trolls, which sell assorted Norwegian goods, including clothing, candy, and statuettes of Norse gods and trolls.

  • June 28, 1988: Disney's Grand Floridian Resort & Spa Opens

    This Victorian themed luxury hotel and spa is located in the Magic Kingdom area, and can be easily accessed from the park by monorail or boat. Designed to be Walt Disney World's signature resort, the hotel's main building features a five story lobby, a replica cage elevator, stained glass domes, and Italian marble floors with inlays of various Disney characters. The Grand Floridian Resort is also home to Victoria & Albert's, an AAA Five Diamond Award-winning restaurant with a menu created nightly and an award-winning wine cellar.

  • July 5, 1988: Maelstrom Opens in the Norway Pavilion

    This log flume dark ride, located in the Norway pavilion in Epcot's World Showcase, took riders through scenes featuring audio-animatronic figures representing Norway's history and culture. The ride vehicles were patterned after longships and went forwards, backwards, and up a lift hill. The ride closed on October 5, 2014, to be replaced by an attraction based on the film Frozen.

  • October 1, 1988: Disney's Caribbean Beach Resort Opens

    Located in the Epcot resort area, this Caribbean-themed hotel contains six areas named after islands in the Caribbean: Martinique, Barbados, Jamaica, Aruba, Trinidad North, and Trinidad South. At the center of the resort is Barefoot Bay, a 45-acre lake whose shores feature beaches, hammocks, and other activities such as beach volleyball.

  • May 1, 1989: Disney-MGM Studios Opens

    The third theme park to open at Walt Disney World, Disney-MGM Studios is dedicated to the facets of show business, including film, television, music, and theatre. The park opened as part theme park and part production studio, with animated features such as Mulan, Lilo & Stitch, and Brother Bear being produced there. Several sound stages were used for both Disney and outside projects. In the 2000's, these production facilities began to shut down, and eventually the park was no longer used for any filming or production. In January 2008, MGM branding was removed from the park, which was renamed Disney's Hollywood Studios. A major expansion was announced in 2015, with Toy Story Land and Star Wars Land being announced for the park.

  • June 1, 1989: Disney's Typhoon Lagoon Opens

    Disney's second water park, Typhoon Lagoon is themed to a typhoon that wreaked havoc upon a formerly pristine tropical paradise. The centerpiece of the park is "Miss Tilly," a shrimp boat impaled upon a mountain named "Mount Mayday" that erupts a 50-foot (15 m) geyser of water every half hour, right before the bells of the watch sound on Miss Tilly. The park operates year-round, with an annual closure for maintenance.

  • June 23, 1989: Delta Dreamflight Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This attraction, located in Magic Kingdom's Tomorrowland, was sponsored by Delta Air Lines. Using a basic Omnimover ride system, Dreamflight was a pop-up book version of the history of flight using simplistic sets, some Audio-Animatronics and projection effects. The attraction closed on January 5, 1998 and was replaced by Buzz Lightyear's Space Ranger Spin.

  • August 25, 1989: Indiana Jones Epic Stunt Spectacular! Debuts

    This show, located in the Echo Lake section of Disney's Hollywood Studios, is based on many of the popular stunt scenes from the film Raiders of the Lost Ark. It is executively produced by George Lucas and directed by Jerry Rees, with stunt coordination by Glenn Randall. As part of the show, selected audience members 18 and older are invited to participate as extras in certain scenes.

  • October 19, 1989: Wonders of Life Pavilion Opens at Epcot

    This pavilion, which was centered around health and fitness, housed two major attractions (Cranium Command and Body Wars), as well as other entertainment and interactive attractions. Cranium Command, a theater show with audio-animatronic actors and a movie, explained the functions of the brain and its interaction with the human body. Body Wars was a motion simulator ride taking guests on a Fantastic Voyage-like trip through the bloodstream. The pavilion moved to seasonal operation beginning on January 4, 2004 and closed permanently on January 1, 2007. The building is now used for special events and festivals.

  • November 5, 1990: Disney's Yacht Club Resort Opens

    This Epcot-area resort, located near Crescent Lake, features a nautical theme. Guests at the resort can access Epcot (via the International Gateway) or Hollywood Studios by walking or by boat. Amenities at the resort include several restaurants, pools (including the popular Stormalong Bay), an arcade, a beach, and a health and fitness center.

  • November 19, 1990: Disney's Beach Club Resort Opens

    Located in the Crescent Lake area adjacent to Epcot, this resort is themed after seaside cottages found in Newport at the start of the 20th century. Boat transportation is available to Epcot and Hollywood Studios, and walking paths are also available for guests who choose not to use the boats. The resort features several restaurants and shares some amenities with the nearby Yacht Club, including one of the nation's largest sand bottom pools, Stormalong Bay.

  • May 16, 1991: Jim Henson's Muppet*Vision 3D opens at Disney-MGM Studios

    This attraction, located in the Muppets Courtyard area of the park, features a 4D film of Kermit the Frog guiding park guests on a tour through Muppet Studios, while the Muppets prepare their sketch acts to demonstrate their new breakthrough in 3D film technology. The attraction incorporates in-theater 4D effects, such as Audio-Animatronics, lighting, projections, smoke, soap bubbles, and a live full-bodied performer, after the tour unravels when Dr. Bunsen Honeydew's experimental 3D sprite, Waldo, causes mayhem. The show was one of the final Muppets projects with the involvement of Jim Henson.

  • May 17, 1991: Disney's Port Orleans Resort Opens

    This resort was designed to reflect the style and architecture of New Orleans' French Quarter. Recreation at the resort includes the Doubloon Lagoon swimming pool, a hot tub, a children's playground, a food court (where beignets are available), and several lounges. In 2001, the resort was renamed Disney's Port Orleans - French Quarter.

  • October 1, 1991: SpectroMagic Parade Premieres at Magic Kingdom

    This nighttime parade, which replaced Main Street Electrical Parade, stars Mickey Mouse, along with the SpectroMagic Spectromen, who together create the power of SpectroMagic. The parade ran through May 1999, before being retired. The parade returned for a second run from April 2001 to June 2010, when it was permanently retired.

  • November 22, 1991: Beauty and the Beast Live on Stage Debuts at Disney-MGM Studios

    This Broadway-style musical plays at the Theater of the Stars in the Sunset Boulevard area of the park. It is based on the popular animated film Beauty and the Beast (the show opened on the same day as the film's premiere), including many of the original songs and characters.

  • December 20, 1991: Disney's Vacation Club Resort Opens

    This resort, renamed Disney's Old Key West Resort in 1996, was the first Disney Vacation Club Timeshare Resort. The accommodations include kitchen facilities and a laundry room, and the rooms are designed in a Key West theme. The resort is located near Disney Springs and is served by bus and watercraft transportation.

  • January 7, 1992: Voyage of the Little Mermaid debuts at Disney-MGM Studios

    This live show tells the abridged version of the story of the 1989 film The Little Mermaid. Along with a mix of live actors and puppets, the show features effects such as light and laser projections on the auditorium walls and light rain over the audience. The show plays in the Animation Courtyard Theater.

  • February 2, 1992: Disney's Dixie Landings Resort Opens

    This resort, which was renamed Disney's Port Orleans Resort - Riverside in 2001, contains two sections, Alligator Bayou and Magnolia Bend (which, as of 2012, includes Royal Rooms). Amenities at the resort include a recreation area called Ol' Man Island (which features a swimming pool, a hot tub, and children's play area, a pool bar, and a fishing hole), the Boatwright’s Dining Hall table-service restaurant, River Roost Lounge, and a food court.

  • October 2, 1992: Splash Mountain Opens

    This log flume dark ride, based on the controversial 1946 film Song of the South, tells the story of Br'er Rabbit, the protagonist, a mischievous hero who leaves his home in search of adventure. The attractions features animated scenes, well-known songs, and a series of drops, including a climactic steep drop into a "briar patch", before ending in an indoor finale.

  • January 16, 1994: Inaugural Walt Disney World Marathon

    This 26.2 mile race launched the Disney Endurance Series, now known as runDisney and consisting of 9 race weekends at three different Disney resorts on two continents. Over 5,000 runners participated in the event, which featured a course that entered all three Disney parks. The current marathon is the only course that enters all four Disney parks, and the weekend now includes a 5K, a 10K, and a half marathon.

  • January 30, 1994: CommuniCore Pavilion Closes

    This pavilion, which opened with Epcot on October 1, 1982, was pavilion dedicated to technological advances. Having debuted at the dawn of the modern computer era, the emphasis throughout CommuniCore was primarily on educating the public about computers. The feature exhibit was a tour through EPCOT Computer Central, the computer hub of EPCOT Center that ran nearly everything throughout the park. The pavilion closed to be replaced by Innoventions, a more eclectic, wild, and corporate-driven take on a Science and Technology pavilion.

  • April 29, 1994: Disney's All-Star Sports Resort Opens

    This resort, Disney's first value resort, was the first of three all-star themed resorts. The resort features five sports-themed areas - Touchdown!, Center Court, Home Run Hotel, Surfs Up!, and Hoops Hotel. A food court features a variety of food options, and the Sport Goofy merchandise location sells resort-themed gifts, Disney products, and ESPN-themed merchandise.

  • May 28, 1994: Disney's Wilderness Lodge Opens

    This hotel is themed with an atmosphere of the Pacific Northwest, and features both natural and Native American elements. The main building was modeled after the Old Faithful Inn in Yellowstone National Park. An artificial geyser and hot springs are located on the resort grounds.

  • July 8, 1994: The Legend of the Lion King Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This stage show, which retold the story of the animated film The Lion King using life size puppets, replaced Magic Journeys in the Fantasyland Theater. The attraction closed on February 23, 2002, to be replaced by Mickey's PhilharMagic.

  • July 22, 1994: The Twilight Zone Tower of Terror Opens

    This drop tower dark ride, located in the Sunset Boulevard area of Disney's Hollywood Studios, is inspired by the television series, The Twilight Zone, and takes place in the fictional Hollywood Tower Hotel in Hollywood, California. The ride experience includes a series of drops in an elevator, and random computer selection is used to ensure a unique series of drops on every ride, leading to the ride's slogan of "Never the Same Fear Twice!"

  • September 29, 1994: Innoventions Opens in Epcot's Future World

    Innoventions, a portmanteau of the words "innovation" and "invention," focuses on technological advancements and their practical applications in everyday life. Innoventions has hosted a number of science and technology focused exhibits and is currently hosting Colortopia, an exhibit presented by Glidden that focuses on the way color affects our lives. The pavilion originally included two sections, Innoventions East and Innoventions West, but Innoventions West closed in 2015 and is currently being used for character experiences.

  • November 22, 1994: Disney's All-Star Music Resort Opens

    This value resort, the first to include family suites, features giant icons that pay homage to classic music genres—including Broadway show tunes, calypso, country, jazz, and rock n' roll. Dining options include a food court, poolside bar, and in-room pizza delivery. Maestro Mickey's is a gift shop that sells souvenirs and snacks.

  • January 21, 1995: Circle of Life: An Environmental Fable Opens at Epcot

    This film, shown in the Harvest Theater in The Land pavilion, features Simba, Timon, and Pumbaa from The Lion King explaining how some actions are harmful to nature. This lesson is explained with a mixture of live-action and animated footage. It replaced Symbiosis, a similarly themed film.

  • April 1, 1995: Disney's Blizzard Beach Opens

    This water park is themed to a ski resort created after a freak snowstorm which later melted. The majority of the major attractions at the park are hosted atop of Mount Gushmore, an artificial hill with an elevation of 90 feet, where the attractions are divided into three areas - Green Slope, Purple Slope, and Red Slope. The park's flagship attraction, Summit Plummet, is located on Green Slope. There are also attractions at ground level, as well as food and beverage areas.

  • June 20, 1995: ExtraTERRORestrial Alien Encounter Opens

    This "theater-in-the-round" attraction, located in Magic Kingdom's Tomorrowland, opened as part of Magic Kingdom's New Tomorrowland. It was a darkly humorous science-fiction experience, which took mostly in the dark and primarily used audio effects. The attraction closed on October 23, 2003, and was replaced by Stitch's Great Escape.

  • November 28, 1995: Walt Disney World Speedway Opens

    This racing facility, designed primarily as a venue for the Indy 200 at Walt Disney World, was primarily used as a venue for the Richard Petty Driving Experience, and the Indy Racing Experience, programs that allows fans to drive or ride in real race cars. The track was no longer used for major racing events after the 2000 racing season, and it closed permanently on August 9, 2015.

  • January 2, 1996: World of Motion Closes

    This attraction which was sponsored by General Motors, opened with Epcot in 1982 and was a humorous look into the history of transportation. Visitors would board moving four to six person Omnimover vehicles, and would be taken through scenes that were populated with Audio-Animatronic figures and also projection effects. The attraction closed to make room for Test Track, which was also sponsored by GM.

  • July 1, 1996: Disney's BoardWalk Resort Opens

    This Epcot-area resort and entertainment district is themed to early 1900's Atlantic City. The entertainment district includes restaurants, nightlife, and shopping. It is located directly across the lake from the Yacht and Beach Club resorts, and both Epcot and Hollywood Studios are accessible by boat or walking path.

  • September 15, 1997: Ellen's Energy Adventure Opens in Epcot's Future World

    This energy-centered attraction, which replaced the opening day attraction "Universe of Energy," stars Ellen DeGeneres, Bill Nye "The Science Guy", Alex Trebek, Johnny Gilbert, and Jamie Lee Curtis. It takes a light-hearted look at various energy resources, how energy is produced, the history of energy production, and the search for new energy resources. The total attraction experience lasts 45 minutes, and features three films and a primeval diorama.

  • October 1, 1996: The Barnstormer at Goofy's Wiseacre Farm Opens

    This junior roller coaster, located in the Storybook Circus section of Magic Kingdom's Fantasyland, appeared to be an airplane school taught by Goofy. The story behind this ride included guests flying in Goofy's homemade airplane as it swooped, twisted and turned. The attraction was rethemed as part of Magic Kingdom's Fantasyland expansion and reopened in March 2012 as The Barnstormer Starring The Great Goofini.

  • March 28, 1997: Disney's Wide World of Sports Opens

    This multi-sport athletic complex, renamed the ESPN Wide World of Sports Complex in 2010, features a baseball stadium and fields, softball fields, multi-purpose arenas, multi-purpose fields, a tennis complex, a cross-country course, and a track and field complex. During runDisney events, WWOS hosts the Health and Fitness Expo. WWOS's Champion Stadium is currently the spring training home of the Atlanta Braves, who have announced plans to move their spring training elsewhere.

  • August 1, 1997: Disney's Coronado Springs Resort Opens

    This moderate resort, located in the Animal Kingdom resort area, has a American colonial Spanish and southwest American theme. It is Walt Disney World's first moderate resort with a convention center, and contains a cafeteria-style restaurant, a formal dinner restaurant (Maya Grill), a gift shop, an arcade, one large pool, three quiet pools, a salon, fitness center, and a dance club.

  • January 11, 1998: Inaugural Donald Half Marathon

    Part of runDisney's Marathon Weekend, this 13.1 mile race was held on the same day as the Walt Disney World Marathon. For eight years the two races would be run on the same day, until 2006, when the Half Marathon was moved to Saturday, leading to the inaugural Goofy “Race and a Half” Challenge (which involved running the half and full marathons on consecutive days).

  • April 5, 1998: The Enchanted Tiki Room (Under New Management) Opens

    This show, which replaced the opening day attraction The Enchanted Tiki Room, was hosted by Iago from Aladdin and Zazu from The Lion King. It closed on January 12, 2011, and was replaced by a modified room of the original Enchanted Tiki Room.

  • April 22, 1998: Disney's Animal Kingdom Park Celebrates Opening Day

    This park, the fourth theme park to open at Walt Disney World, is dedicated and themed entirely around the natural environment and animal conservation. Disney's Animal Kingdom is accredited by the Association of Zoos and Aquariums and the World Association of Zoos and Aquariums, and is engaged in research and conservation efforts involving its animal species.

  • June 19, 1998: DisneyQuest Opens at Disney Springs

    This indoor interactive theme park was meant to be the first of a chain of DisneyQuest locations across the country, but was in reality the first of only two locations to be built (the other location, Chicago, would open in 1999 and close in 2001). Attractions include virtual rides and classic arcade games. DisneyQuest is currently scheduled to close on July 2, 2017, to make room for a new NBA-themed attraction.

  • September 7, 1998: Mr. Toad's Wild Ride Closes

    This opening-day Magic Kingdom dark ride, based on Disney's adaptation of The Wind in the Willows, contained two separate ride experiences, based on where you boarded. Mr. Toad's Wild Ride was replaced with The Many Adventures of Winnie the Pooh. Minor tributes to the ride can be found in Disney World, including paintings of Mr. Toad and Moley within the Many Adventures of Winnie the Pooh attraction and a statue of Toad in a pet cemetery outside of Haunted Mansion.

  • October 7, 1998: Buzz Lightyear Space Ranger Spin Opens

    In this shooting game, located in Magic Kingdom's Tomorrowland, guests help Buzz Lighyear defeat the Evil Emperor Zurg. Guests use laser cannons to shoot targets and score points, with the maximum score being 999,999 (known as "Galactic Hero").

  • October 15, 1998: Fantasmic! Opens at Hollywood Studios

    This nightime show, which is held in the Hollywood Hills Amphitheater in the Sunset Boulevard area of the park, centers on a voyage through Mickey Mouse's imagination. The show uses fireworks, characters, live actors, water effects, pyrotechnics, lasers, music, audio-animatronics, decorated boat floats, and mist screen projections, and culminates in a battle with Disney Villains.

  • January 9, 1999: Horizons Closes

    This attraction, which was located on the eastern side of Epcot's Future World, used an omnimover system to take guests past show scenes depicting visions of the future. The attraction officially opened with the park on October 1, 1983, closed in December 1994, and reopened in December 1995 before closing permanently on January 9, 1999, after which the attraction was dismantled and its structure demolished to make room for Mission:SPACE.

  • January 15, 1999: Disney's All-Star Movies Resort Opens

    This value resort, located in the Animal Kingdom resort area, is the last of Disney's three All-Star resorts. The resort features areas themed after 5 movies: 101 Dalmations, Fantasia, Toy Story, The Mighty Ducks, and Herbie: The Love Bug.

  • March 17, 1999: Test Track Opens at Epcot

    This high-speed attraction, sponsored by General Motors, was meant to simulate the testing that concept cars go through before their release. The attraction would close for refurbishment in 2012 and reopen with a new theme (designing a car and testing its capability, efficiency, responsiveness, and power) and a new sponsor (Chevrolet).

  • June 4, 1999: The Many Adventures of Winnie the Pooh Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This attraction, located in Fantasyland, is a dark ride based upon the film of the same name, itself based on the Winnie-the-Pooh books by A. A. Milne.Riders will see giant storybook pages and scenes from the story, and experience some physical effects, including bouncing. The attraction ends with a final scene of characters celebrating the end of the rain, Pooh enjoying honey, and a final storybook page - "The End." A recent refurbishment added an interactive queue area for kids.

  • July 29, 1999: Rock 'n' Roller Coaster Starring Aerosmith Opens

    Located in Sunset Boulevard area of Disney's Hollywood Studios, this enclosed steel roller coaster features three inversions on over one mile of track, all set to Aerosmith music.

  • October 1, 1999: IllumiNations 2000: Reflections of Earth Debuts at Epcot

    This show, which opened as part of the Walt Disney World Millenium Celebration, utilizes fireworks, pyrotechnics, water fountains, fire effects, lasers, and searchlights to create a visual production on the park's World Showcase Lagoon.

  • October 1, 1999: Journey into YOUR Imagination Opens

    This attraction, located in Epcot's Future World, took guests on a tour of the fictional Imagination Institute and had them be test subjects for the new invention, the Imagination Scanner. The attraction featured reduced appearances from Figment, the mascot of the pavilion, who would was featured in the original version of this attraction. This version closed in October 2001, and reopened in 2002 as Journey into Imagination with Figment.

  • November 15, 2000: The Villas at Disney's Wilderness Lodge Opens

    The villas, a Disney Vacation Club property, will later be expanded and renamed Boulder Ridge Villas. The expansion will include waterside villas, new amenities, and new dining facilities.

  • April 1, 2001: SpectroMagic Debuts at Magic Kingdom

    The second incarnation of this popular nighttime parade runs through June 5, 2010, when it closes permanently.

  • April 7, 2001: Who Wants To Be A Millionaire – Play It! Debuts at Disney-MGM Studios

    The attraction, a modified version of the Who Wants to Be a Millionaire television game show, closed on August 20, 2004.

  • April 16, 2001: Disney's Animal Kingdom Lodge Opens

    The resort, whose main building is renamed Jambo House when a Disney Vacation Club property opens as part of the resort, offers an opportunity to view wildlife in a savanna-type environment outside of public areas and certain guest rooms.

  • May 24, 2001: The Magic Carpets of Aladdin Opens

    The attraction, located in Magic Kingdom's Adventureland, is an aerial carousel ride that allows riders to control both the height of their carpet and the amount of tilt.

  • October 1, 2001: Share A Dream Come True Parade Debuts at Magic Kingdom

    This afternoon parade, which was renamed as the Disney Dreams Come True Parade in 2006 and again as Celebrate A Dream Come True in 2009, ended its final run on January 4th, 2014.

  • October 1, 2001: Cinderella's Surprise Celebration Debuts

    This show, which took place on a stage in front of Cinderella Castle at Magic Kingdom, went on hiatus on February 26, 2005, and never returned from that hiatus.

  • October 1, 2001: Walt Disney: One Man’s Dream Opens at Disney-MGM Studios

    This interactive gallery attraction takes guests inside the world of Walt Disney and features artifacts from his life, as well as models of various Disney parks around the world. The attraction ends with a short film of Walt Disney's life.

  • October 1, 2001: Playhouse Disney – Live on Stage Debuts at Disney-MGM Studios

    The show, which features beloved characters from Disney Junior shows, will later be renamed Disney Junior - Live on Stage!

  • November 1, 2001: TriceraTop Spin Opens at Animal Kingdom

    This aerial carousel attraction, located in Dinoland U.S.A., allows riders to control both up/down and forward/backward motion.

  • March 31, 2002: Primeval Whirl Opens at Animal Kingdom

    This attraction, a steel wild mouse roller coaster located in the Chester and Hester's Dino-Rama portion of Dinoland U.S.A., is themed to time travel and to the meteor which is believed to have caused the extinction of the dinosaurs.

  • June 2, 2002: Journey into Imagination with Figment Opens at Epcot

    This attraction, the third incarnation of an imagination-themed attraction located in the Imagination! pavilion in Epcot's future world, features a small purple dragon named Figment, who is currently voiced by Muppeteer Dave Goelz.

  • July 1, 2002: Disney's Beach Club Villas, a Disney Vacation Club Resort, Opens

    The villas, located in the Epcot resort area, share their amenities, including Stormalong Bay (one of the most popular pools on Disney property), with the Beach Club and Yacht Club resorts.

  • May 22, 2003: Reflections of China Opens at Epcot

    This Circle-Vision 360° movie, located in the China pavilion in World Showcase, replaced the opening day attraction Wonders of China. The show is narrated by an actor playing Li Bai, an ancient Chinese poet, who takes the viewer on a tour of the Chinese countryside and historical structures and buildings.

  • August 15, 2003: Mission:Space Opens at Epcot

    This motion simulator attraction, which simulates what an astronaut might experience aboard a spacecraft on a mission to Mars, currently offers two experiences: Orange Team, which is more intense (and reflects the opening day attraction), and Green Team, which does not spin and is therefore a less intense experience.

  • October 8, 2003: Mickey's PhilharMagic Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This attraction, which is located in Fantasyland, starts Donald Duck and Mickey Mouse, and features a 4-D adventure through some of Disney Animation's greatest musical movie moments.

  • October 9, 2003: Wishes: A Magical Gathering of Disney Dreams Debuts at Magic Kingdom

    This nighttime spectacular, hosted by Jiminy Cricket and the Blue Fairy, features fireworks launched over both Cinderella Castle and Beast's Castle, changing colors on Cinderella Castle, and a special appearance by Tinker Bell. The show's last performance with be May 11, 2017, as it is retired to make room for a new nighttime spectacular.

  • December 14, 2003: Disney's Pop Century Resort Opens

    This value resort, located near ESPN's Wide World of Sports, celebrates pop culture from 5 decades: the 50's, 60's, 70's, 80's, and 90's. A second part of the resort, celebrating the first half of the 20th century, was planned but never built.

  • May 17, 2004: Disney's Saratoga Springs Resort & Spa Opens

    This Disney Vacation Club property, located near Disney Springs, was inspired by the city of Saratoga Springs, New York, and contains 18 buildings of villas, as well as 60 Treehouse Villas. It is one of two resorts built exclusively for Disney Vacation Club villas (however, the villas are bookable as regular hotel rooms).

  • November 16, 2004: Turtle Talk with Crush Opens

    This interactive attraction, housed in the Living Seas pavillion, allows audience members to interact with the sea turtle Crush, a character from Pixar's Finding Nemo. Crush answers questions from the audience about life in the sea and other topics.

  • November 18, 2004: Stitch's Great Escape Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This attraction, which features the title alien from Lilo & Stitch, is located in the building that formerly housed ExtraTERRORestrial Alien Encounter. It currently operates as a seasonal attraction.

  • March 15, 2005: Crush ‘n’ Gusher Opens at Typhoon Lagoon

    This ride was the first water coaster to be built for a Disney water park, and features three different slides, the "Banana Blaster", "Coconut Crusher", or "Pineapple Plunger," which range from 410 to 420 feet in length, but all send riders to a plunge into the "Hideaway Bay" pool.

  • May 5, 2005: Soarin' Opens at Epcot

    This attraction, located in The Land pavillion, originally took riders on an aerial tour of California. The attraction closed for a several month refurbishment in 2016, and shortly after it reopened, on June 17, 2016, the film was changed to an aerial tour of the world called Soarin' Around the World.

  • May 15, 2005: Lights, Motors, Action! Extreme Stunt Show Debuts

    This stunt show, located in the Backlot area of Disney-MGM Studios (later Hollywood Studios), was brought over from the Walt Disney Studios Park in Paris as part of the Happiest Celebration on Earth event (celebrating 50 years of Disney Parks). The show closed in April of 2016 to make room for expansions at Hollywood Studios.

  • December 9, 2005: Journey into Narnia: Creating the Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe Opens

    This stand and watch attraction operated at Disney-MGM Studios from December 2005 to January 1, 2008, when it closed to make room for Journey into Narnia: Prince Caspian, based on a newer installment in the same film franchise.

  • January 21, 2006: Tarzan Rocks! Closes at Animal Kindom

    This show, which was held in the Theater in the Wild in Dinoland U.S.A., featured rollerskating monkeys along with Tarzan, Jane and Terk. It also featured songs from the 1999 film Tarzan.

  • April 7, 2006: Expedition Everest Opens at Animal Kingdom

    This steel coaster, located in the Asia section of Animal Kingdom, is themed around the Yeti hiding in Mount Everest. It is the tallest artificial mountain in the Disney Parks, and was the first Disney roller coaster to switch between forward and backward sections during a single ride.

  • January 1, 2007: Finding Nemo – The Musical Debuts

    This show, which is located in the Theater in the Wild in Animal Kingdom's Dinoland U.S.A., is based on the popular Pixar film Finding Nemo and features new songs written by Robert Lopez and Kristen Anderson-Lopez, as well as puppets, dancers, acrobats and animated backdrops.

  • January 24, 2007: The Seas with Nemo & Friends Opens at Epcot

    This area, known from opening day until August 21, 2005 as "The Living Seas," houses an aquarium, a dark ride themed around Finding Nemo, and an interactive attraction themed around the sea turtle Crush.

  • April 2, 2007: Monsters, Inc. Laugh Floor Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This attraction, located in Tomorrowland, invites guests into the world of the Pixar film Monsters, Inc., where they are entertained at Monstropolis' first comedy club. Guests are given the ability to submit jokes to be used in the show, and guests have an opportunity to interact with the show.

  • April 6, 2007: Gran Fiesta Tour Starring The Three Caballeros Opens

    This attraction, located in Epcot's Mexico pavillion, is a musical dark boat ride that replaced El Rio del Tiempo. It was the first World Showcase attraction to feature Disney characters based on an existing property, and features Panchito Pistoles, José Carioca, and Donald Duck, from the 1944 film The Three Caballeros.

  • September 10, 2007: Bibbidi Bobbidi Boutique Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This enchanted beauty salon, located inside Cinderella Castle, offers serveral magical makover packages where a "Fairy Godmother in Training" will transform young children into princesses or knights. This is the second Bibbidi Bobbidi Boutique location at Walt Disney World, with the first location inside Downtown Disney's World of Disney store.

  • January 7, 2008: Disney-MGM Studios Becomes Disney's Hollywood Studios

    This name change, announced in August 2007, marked the end of a partnership between Disney and Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer. The original name-change announcement also included the announcement of several new attractions, which opened in 2007 and 2008.

  • February 19, 2008: Spaceship Earth Reopens After Refurbishment

    The attraction, located in Epcot's famous geodesic sphere, reopens after a 7-month refurbishment with a new sponsor, Siemens, and a new narrator, Dame Judi Dench. The update also included new scenes, modifications to existing scenes, some new costumes, lighting, and props, a new musical score by Bruce Broughton.

  • May 31, 2008: Toy Story Midway Mania! Opens at Hollywood Studios

    This attraction, located in the Pixar Place section of the park, allows guests wearing 3-D glasses to play five mini-games based on classic carnival midway games. The attraction queue features a large Mr. Potato Head Audio-Animatronics figure that interacts with guests through pre-recorded snippets of dialogue.

  • June 27, 2008: Journey into Narnia: Prince Caspian Opens at Hollywood Studios

    This one-room walkthrough attraction featured trivia, behind-the-scenes footage, sets, clips, art, storyboards, props, and costumes from the film The Chronicles of Narnia: Prince Caspian. It closed on September 10, 2011 and was replaced by The Legend Of Captain Jack Sparrow.

  • February 14, 2009: The American Idol Experience Opens

    This attraction, located at the Hyperion Theater in the Echo Lake section of Hollywood Studios, invited park guests to audition in front of "casting directors" and then live audiences, giving the audiences opportunities to vote for their favorite singers. The final show of each day featured the winners of the previous shows, and the winner of this show was given a "Dream Ticket" that provided the opportunity to bypass the queue at a regional American Idol taping. The attraction closed on August 30, 2014.

  • March 3, 2009: Inaugural Princess Half-Marathon Weekend Begins

    This female-themed race weekend, celebrating princesses of all kind, featured two races in its inagural year, the Royal Family 5K and Princess Half-Marathon, as well as a Health and Fitness Expo.

  • May 1, 2009: Kidani Village opens at Disney's Animal Kingdom Lodge

    Kidani Village, a Disney Vacation Club property, is the second building at Animal Kingdom, and features views of animals in a savanna environment from both public areas and selected rooms.

  • August 4, 2009: Bay Lake Tower at Disney's Contemporary Resort Opens

    This resort, a Disney Vacation Club properly located adjacent to Magic Kingdom, occupies the land formerly occupied by a garden wing of the original A-frame Contemporary Tower. It features its own pool, water feature play area, pool bar, bocce court, Community Hall featuring games and family activities, guest laundry room, but shares some amenities with the Contemporary Resort.

  • October 14, 2009: The Sum of All Thrills Opens at Epcot

    This attraction, located within the Innoventions pavillion, was a virtual roller coaster that allowed park guests custom-design their own thrill ride using mathematical tools, an innovative touch-screen table and a robotic simulator. It closed permanently on September 14, 2016.

  • May 9, 2010: Honey, I Shrunk the Audience Closes

    Located in Epcot's Future World, this 4-D film is a spin off of the movie Honey, I Shrunk the Kids. The attraction opened on November 21, 1994, and was replaced by a new version of the attraction it originally replaced, Captain EO.

  • June 1, 2010: Cinderella's Golden Carrousel Becomes Prince Charming Regal Carrousel

    The carousel, located in Magic Kingdom's Fantasyland, is renamed to better fit the story behind the attraction. According to the official backstory, the carousel was inspired by a device built by Prince Charming to assist him in training for the ring-spearing event at jousting matches. A second carousel was built closer to the castle to allow villagers to try their hand at this device. With this new backstory, park visitors were invited to test their horsemanship skills and to enjoy their own happy ending while riding the carousel.

  • July 2, 2010: Captain EO Tribute Opens at Epcot

    This 4-D film, a new presentation of the film Captain EO (which originally ran at Epcot from 1986-1994), was brought back to Disney Parks around the world after the death of its star, Michael Jackson. The film was directed by Francis Ford Coppola and produced by George Lucas. Captain EO Tribute closed on December 6, 2015, to make room for a new attraction featuring Disney and Pixar short films.

  • October 2, 2010: Inaugural runDisney Wine & Dine Half-Marathon Weekend Begins

    This race weekend, themed around Epcot's International Food and Wine Festival, featured Mickey's Halloween Family Fun Run 5K and the Wine and Dine Half Marathon, as well as two-day expo and a Food & Wine Festival Finish Line Party. With a 10 PM start, this was runDisney's first nighttime half marathon, and offered a relay option for runners who did not wish to complete the first 13.1 miles.

  • May 20, 2011: Star Tours: The Adventure Continues Opens at Hollywood Studios

    This 3D motion simulator, an updated version of the attraction Star Tours, takes guests inside the world of Star Wars. Guests travel through locations seen in Star Wars Episodes I - VII as droids C-3PO and R2-D2 attempt to safely return a rebel spy to the Rebel Alliance.

  • August 15, 2011: The Enchanted Tiki Room Reopens at Magic Kingdom

    This show, which originally ran from October 1971 to September 1997, is a slightly edited version of the classic Disneyland attraction. The show features over 150 talking, singing and dancing birds, flowers, a magic fountain, tiki drummers and tiki totem poles, all singing the attraction's signature tunes, including "The Tiki Tiki Tiki Room" by the Sherman Brothers.

  • May 18, 2012: Sounds Dangerous! Closes at Hollywood Studios

    This audio show, located in Echo Lake section of the park, opened on April 22, 1999. The show starred comedian Drew Carey and relied almost entirely on audio effects with few visuals.

  • May 31, 2012: Disney's Art of Animation Resort Opens

    The value resort, located near ESPN's Wide World of Sports Complex and built on the land that was originally intended for the "Classic Years" section of Pop Century resort, features both family suites and motel-style standard rooms, divided into four themed areas. The Finding Nemo, Cars, and The Lion King areas all house family suites, while The Little Mermaid section contains standard hotel rooms. The resort has a food court, souvenir shop, arcade room, pool bar, and three pools, and is connected to the Pop Century Resort by the Generation Gap Bridge.

  • October 4, 2012: Pete's Silly Sideshow and Big Top Souvenirs Open in Magic Kingdom's Storybook Circus

    Pete's Silly Sideshow, a character meet and greet area, features The Astounding Donaldo (Donald Duck), The Great Goofini (Goofy), Madame Daisy Fortuna (Daisy Duck), and Minnie Magnifique (Minnie Mouse). Big Top Souvenirs is a large circus tent that sells various souvenirs, including various Disney-themed caramel apples.

  • November 9, 2012: Be Our Guest Restaurant Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This restaurant, located in Beast's Castle and themed around the animated film Beauty and the Beast, contains three rooms in which guests can eat: the West Wing, the Ballroom, and the Rose Gallery. The restaurant is known for its signature "grey stuff" and was the first restaurant in Magic Kingdom to serve alcohol (at dinner only).

  • December 6, 2012: New Fantasyland Opens at Magic Kingdom

    A special ceremony celebrated the official opening of Magic Kingdom's New Fantasyland, an expansion and renovation originally announced in 2009. New attractions that opened on this day included Ariel's Grotto (a meet and greet area), Under the Sea: Journey of the Little Mermaid (an dark ride that tells the story of The Little Mermaid), and Enchanted Tales with Belle (an interactive show where guests help tell the story of Belle and the Beast).

  • December 6, 2012: The Legend of Captain Jack Sparrow Opens at Hollywood Studios

    This walk-through attraction, located in the area that formerly housed Journey into Narnia: Prince Caspian, follows the story of Captain Jack Sparrow from the Pirates of the Caribbean film series. The attraction closed on November 6, 2014, to make room for upcoming expansions at the park.

  • March 8, 2013: Tangled Rest Area Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This rest area, located in Fantasyland, allows guests to rest in a highly-themed area featuring Rapunzel's Tower, lanterns, wanted posters for ruffians, and many other elements from the film Tangled. In addition to restrooms, the area contains charging areas, benches, and a scavenger hunt to find hidden Pascals.

  • September 17, 2013: Princess Fairytale Hall Opens

    This meet and greet area, located in Magic Kingdom's Fantasyland, allows guests to meet various Disney Princesses. Currently, Cinderella, Elena, Rapunzel and Tiana are meeting in this location, but the princesses are subject to change. In the past, Anna and Elsa have met in this location, as have Snow White and Aurora.

  • October 23, 2013: The Villas at Disney's Grand Floridian Resort & Spa Opens

    This Disney Vacation Club property, located in the Magic Kingdom resort area. The property features its own checkin and concierge areas, but also shares many amenities with the Grand Floridian Resort & Spa. The villas and studios contain subtle theming to Dumbo the Flying Elephant and Mary Poppins.

  • January 9, 2014: Inaugural runDisney Dopey Challenge Begins

    The Dopey Challenge, part of Walt Disney World Marathon Weekend, includes four races over four days - the Family Fun Run 5K, the Walt Disney World 10K (new for 2014), the Walt Disney World Half Marathon, and the Walt Disney World Marathon. After running 48.6 miles, participants will have received a total of 6 medals - 4 individual race medals, the Goofy Race-and-a-Half Challenge medal (for running the Half Marathon and Marathon), and the Dopey Challenge medal. Over 5,000 people complete the Dopey Challenge in its inaugural year.

  • February 22, 2014: Inagural Enchanted 10K Race

    The 2014 Princess Half Marathon Weekend features a new race, the Enchanted 10K, which runs through Epcot and the Epcot resort area. In addition to the new race, a challenge is added to the weekend. The Glass Slipper challenge awards a medal to registered participants who complete both the Enchanted 10K and Princess Half Marathon. Also introduced this year is the Pink Coast-to-Coast Medal, awarded to runners who complete the Tinker Bell Half Marathon at Disneyland and the Princess Half Marathon at Walt Disney World.

  • May 28, 2014: Seven Dwarfs Mine Train Opens at Magic Kingdom

    This steel roller coaster, the last attraction to open as part of Magic Kingdom's New Fantasyland expansion, is themed around the classic animated film Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs. The attraction, which contains both outdoor and indoor elements, operates with a new ride system with "tilting" vehicles, and features a mixture of new audio-animatronics and figures recycled from a now-closed attraction, Snow White's Scary Adventures.

  • June 1, 2014: Festival of the Lion King at Animal Kingdom Reopens

    This show, which closed when its theater was demolished for an Avatar-themed expansion, is now hosted in the Harambe Theater in the Africa section of the park. Inspired by the film The Lion King, the show uses songs, dance, puppetry and visual effects to portray a tribal celebration in an African savanna setting filled with lions, elephants, giraffes, birds, zebras and gazelles.

  • July 5, 2014: For the First Time in Forever: A Frozen Sing-Along Celebration Debuts

    This musical sing-along show, originally located in the Premiere Theater in the Streets of America section of Disney's Hollywood Studios, tells the story of the 2013 film Frozen with the help of new characters, the Royal Historians of Arendelle. In June 2015, the show moved to a new location, the Hyperion Theater in Echo Lake (formerly the home of the American Idol Experience).

  • September 27, 2014: Studio Backlot Tour at Hollywood Studios Closes

    The Studio Backlot Tour, which opened with the park on May 1, 1989, was a combination walking and tram tour of the backlot area of the park. The tour, which was changed and shortened over the years, closed with no announcement of what would replace it. It was eventually announced that the area would be used for expansions to the park.

  • April 1, 2015: Disney's Polynesian Villas & Bungalows Opens

    This newest Disney Vacation Club property, located in the Magic Kingdom reosrt area, features both studios and bungalows. The 'Bora Bora' Bungalows are located over the water, sleep up to 8 guests, and offer a view of Magic Kingdom and a private plunge pool.

  • July 12, 2015: The Magic of Disney Animation at Hollywood Studios Closes

    This attraction, which opened with Disney-MGM Studios on May 1, 1989, and its building originally housed the Orlando, Florida division of Walt Disney Feature Animation. It featured exhibits exploring the history of Disney animation, The Animation Academy (where guests can learn to draw their favorite Disney characters), and character meet and greets. No announcement about the future of the building was made when the attraction closed, but the building now houses the Star Wars Launch Bay.

  • September 29, 2015: Downtown Disney Becomes Disney Springs

    Walt Disney World's shopping, dining, and entertainment complex is given a new name and a new theme as part of a major expansion project. The complex is divided into four areas, Marketplace, The Landing, Town Center, and West Side, and features many new dining and shopping locations as part of its expansion and rebranding.

  • December 1, 2015: Jedi Training: Trials of the Temple Debuts at Hollywood Studios

    This show, an update of the Jedi Training Academy, allows children to train with a Jedi master and Apprentice. After training, the participants are given the opportunity to fight against Star Wars villains, including Darth Vader and The Seventh Sister (from Star Wars Rebels).

  • December 5, 2015: Star Wars Launch Bay Opens

    This attraction, located in the Animation Courtyard area of Hollywood Studios, features props from the Star Wars series of films. Additionally, character meet and greets are available, and Jawas wander around the pavillion looking to trade goods with guests.

  • December 16, 2015: Jungle Skipper Canteen Opens at Magic Kingdom

    Located in the Adventureland area of the park, this restaurant is themed around the nearby Jungle Cruise attraction. According to its backstory, the skippers of the Jungle Cruise have opened the kitchen at their headquarters to guests. The restaurant contains three rooms: the crew’s colonial-era Mess Hall; the Jungle Room, former family parlor of Dr. Albert Falls himself; and the S.E.A. Room—a once-secret meeting place for the Society of Explorers and Adventurers.

  • December 23, 2015: Disney & Pixar Short Film Festival Opens

    Located in Epcot's Future World, in the theater that formerly housed Captain EO, this attraction includes three Disney and Pixar short films: Get a Horse (Disney), For the Birds (Pixar), and La Luna (Pixar). 4D effects are used to enhance the films.

  • April 2, 2016: Honey, I Shrunk the Kids: Movie Set Adventure Closes

    This kids' play area, locaed in the Streets of America section of Hollywood Studios, featured large movie props and structures inspired by the classic Disney film Honey, I Shrunk the Kids. It closed to make room for expansions at Hollywood Studios.

  • April 14, 2016: Inaugural Star Wars Half Marathon — The Dark Side Weekend Begins

    This race weekend, the second runDisney weekend with a Star Wars theme, included three races: the Star Wars 5K, the Star Wars 10K, and the Star Wars Half Marathon – The Dark Side. Participants who registered for the Dark Side Challenge and completed both the 10K and Half Marathon races received a special challenge t-shirt and medal. Participants who completed both the Star Wars Half Marathon - The Light Side at Disneyland (in January) and the Star Wars Half Marathon - The Dark Side at Walt Disney World received a special Kessel Run-themed medal (shaped like the Millenium Falcon).

  • June 17, 2016: Star Wars: A Galactic Spectacular Debuts at Hollywood studios

    This nighttime spectacular, which replaced Symphony in the Stars: A Galactic Spectacular, features fireworks, projection mapping, fire, lasers, fog effects, and searchlights. Images from the Star Wars films are projected onto The Great Movie Ride's exterior Chinese Theater, and the fireworks and effects are set to music from the films.

  • June 21, 2016: Frozen Ever After Opens at Epcot

    Located in the Norway pavillion in World Showcase, this attraction features scenes inspired by Disney's 2013 animated film Frozen as well as the 2015 animated short Frozen Fever.

  • October 2, 2016: The Muppets Present...Great Moments in American History Premieres

    This live show, located in the Liberty Square area at the Magic Kingdom, is performed daily and features the Muppets presenting abridged accounts of notable milestones in American history, primarily from the Colonial era. The majority of the show unfolds from the second-story windows of the Heritage House, adjacent to the Hall of Presidents attraction.

  • November 5, 2016: Inaugural Wine and Dine 10K Race

    This inaugural race, represented by Chef Remy and added as part of a number of changes to the Wine and Dine Half Marathon Weekend, runs through Epcot and the Epcot resort area. In addition to the 10K, a challenge was added to the weekend, giving participants the opportunity to earn the Lumiere's Two-Course Challenge medal for completing both the 10K and Half Marathon (the half marathon, which had been held on Saturday night in previous years, was moved to Sunday morning).
